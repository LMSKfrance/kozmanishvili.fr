Symmetra Claims Workflow

Lead Product Designer · 2020–2023

Context

Redesigning the claims handling workflow for an insurtech platform serving 100,000+ users across 4 product lines.

Constraints

Enterprise reliability, multi-role access (agents, handlers, admins), high-density data interfaces.

Role

Research lead, IA, design system architecture, and mentorship of 3 junior designers.

Research

Contextual inquiry with 12 claims handlers across 3 rounds of usability testing. Shadowed agents during live processing.

UX Thinking

Reduced 11-step linear flow to 5 steps across 3 screens. Smart defaults and progressive disclosure removed cognitive load.

UI System

High-density data tables, role-based views, semantic status indicators. System scaled across 4 product lines.

Outcome

Claims handling 12→7 min · errors down 50% · support tickets down 40% · onboarding activation +22%.

Clinical Workflow Platform

Product Designer · 2017–2020

Context

Replacing paper-based clinical workflows for a healthcare client where staff had abandoned the digital system.

Constraints

Clinical data sensitivity, GDPR, multi-role system for patients, clinicians, and admin staff.

Role

First designer at the agency. Contextual research, information architecture, interface design.

Research

Contextual inquiry — shadowed nurses and doctors across 3 clinical shifts. Identified paper backups, double entry, and context-switching as root causes.

UX Thinking

Reframed brief from "improve the UI" to "redesign the IA around the patient journey, not the admin org chart."

UI System

Cognitive-load-optimized interfaces for high-pressure clinical moments. Clear hierarchy for time-critical decisions.

Outcome

Full staff adoption · paper backup usage eliminated · admin processing time significantly reduced.
