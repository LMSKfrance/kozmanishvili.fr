01 — The Problem

200+ inconsistent components. Zero shared system.

An internal CRM serving 40+ brokers with no shared design language. Every module felt like a different product — 200+ unique button styles, mixed accent colors between case management and quoting, and no documentation. Engineering rebuilt every UI pattern from scratch, every sprint.

The business cost was measurable: slow handoffs, recurring bugs in production, and a team spending more time debugging UI than building features.

07 — Reflection

What worked, and what I'd do differently.

What worked.
01

Token-first approach

Starting with tokens (incl. the deliberate green/blue split) meant every downstream decision was systematic, not arbitrary. Components became predictable.

02

Engineering partnership

Involving engineers from week one, not at handoff, meant the system was built for production from the start.

03

Design QA

A pre-ship checklist between design and engineering reduced production bugs by 60% and built trust across the team.

What I'd do differently.
01

Document earlier

I started documenting after building. Next time: documentation starts on day one, in parallel with components.

02

User-test the system itself

I tested the product but not the design system's usability for other designers. I'd run system-usability sessions earlier.

03

Version control from the start

Git-style versioning for the Figma library would have saved significant time when components evolved.
